This guideline is designed to assist users in understanding how to interpret solar system size and how to read their TNB electricity bill effectively.
It provides step-by-step instructions on identifying the relevant information on a solar system specification and
explains the key components of a typical TNB bill. The guideline also includes a corresponding calculator to help
users estimate energy production based on system size and calculate electricity costs or savings more accurately based on their usage.

How to Read Your Solar System Size ?

Understanding Your Solar System Size via SolisCloud

When you open your SolisCloud app, the initial page provides an overview of your solar system's performance and key information. Look for the blue circle, which clearly indicates your installed solar system capacity (in kW or kWP).

This capacity represents the maximum power your solar panels can generate under optimal conditions.

Monthly Yield Insight

Scroll down the page and you will see options for Daily, Monthly, Year, and Total. Click on Monthly to see how many kWh your solar panel has produced monthly.

Sun Peak by Area and Roof Angle

This chart shows the monthly sun peak hours for different areas and roof angles, which affect your solar panel performance.

How to Read Your TNB Bill?

Understanding your TNB bill is crucial for managing your electricity costs effectively.
Below, we break down the key components of a typical TNB bill and how to interpret them.
The TNB bill is divided into several sections, each providing important information about your electricity usage and charges.
Here are the main components you should look for:

Here is your Solar Energy that has been stored in TNB. It highlights your remaining balance.

At your TNB Bill second page, you can found this. This section provides an overview of your solar energy generation and consumption.
In this example, you can see that 983kWh from TNB has been used and cost RM442.99.
The solar panels has generated 576kWh of energy, which is valued at RM310.78.
After deducting the solar energy value from the TNB usage cost and adding the service tax and KWTBB,
the total amount payable is RM156.20.
Please note that if the solar energy value is higher than the TNB usage cost,
it will offset the TNB usage cost.
For example, if you used 300kWh from TNB and your solar panels generated 500kWh,
the green color table will only show total 300kWh, remaining 200kWh will be carried forward as your Baki NEM credits.

At the last page of your TNB Bill, you can see this table.
It show the actual kWh that generate by your own solar system and also import form TNB.
The kWh(e) representing the energy generated by your solar system, while kWh(i) represents the energy imported from TNB. In this example, the solar system generated 576 kWh(e) and imported 983 kWh(i) from TNB.
